User Interface Design

CereSprint

A collaborative work made to appeal to younger students. Its purpose is to educate visitors of the Eco House in CERES park about the various features of the house that make it eco-friendly.

These two elements of the application — being an educational resource about the environment, and needing to appeal to kids — were the primary factors in deciding the design of the menu layouts. The colours are bright, with plenty of pastel shades in the palette. Pink flowers were a big part of the gameplay within the app, and were incorporated often into the UI in order to have a symbol that would help make the app and experience memorable.

BreakBuddy

Another collaborative project, the focus of BreakBuddy was to remind people every once in a while to take a break from doomscrolling, giving them little "prompts" with activities they could do. It also had a social aspect, with users being able to share their responses to their prompts, to better encourage them to do the prompts.

Various styles were proposed for this app, but one that was simple, but still colourful and felt casual to match the name of the app, was chosen in the end to decide the overall look and feel of the experience. Those main colours of yellow, green and light blue, especially helped to capture ideas of the outside.
